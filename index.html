Measles Vaccination Rates for Every Kindergarten in California

In 2000, measles was declared eliminated from the United States – largely due to the development of a highly effective vaccine against measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) and a strong national vaccination program that achieved high coverage among children. In recent years, however, the rise of the anti-vaccination movement has left several communities with concerningly low measles vaccination coverage. Experts recommend that at least 92 to 95 percent of a community be vaccinated against measles to protect against outbreaks.
